EU Regulation 2021/1232
-----------------------

Regulation (EU) 2021/1232 is a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council of the EU on a temporary derogation from certain provisions of Directive 2002/58/EC concerning the use of technologies by providers of number-independent interpersonal communications services for the processing of personal and other data for the purpose of combating online child sexual abuse. In scope electronic communication service providers are required to publish an annual transparency report on the processing of personal data under this regulation. This report provides insight into the use of technologies for the purpose of combating online child sexual abuse in the European Union and reporting such content to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC).

EU Digital Services Act (DSA) Transparency Reports
--------------------------------------------------

The DSA is a regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council of the EU on a single market for digital services and amending Directive 2000/31/EC. These reports are prepared annually, beginning in February 2024, pursuant to the requirements of the EU Digital Services Act (DSA). This does not include reports from designated "Very Large" services.
